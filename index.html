Admission Rates Have Dropped Dramatically

2025

From 2020 to 2025, admission rates across all Ivy League schools have declined sharply and remained at historic lows.

This line chart tracks how acceptance rates have evolved over time, with each school represented by a distinct colored line. The smooth curves make trends easy to follow, and hovering over any point reveals the exact admission rate for that year. Use the toggle buttons to show or hide specific schools, and adjust the slider to see data through different years.

03

Financial Aid: Where Sticker Prices Become Actual Prices

Once admitted, families discover the real story: financial aid varies dramatically across schools, making actual costs very different from sticker prices.

This horizontal chart reveals two critical factors that determine actual affordability. The line length shows average aid amounts, while circle size indicates what percentage of demonstrated financial need each school meets. Schools with larger circles meet more of students' need, significantly reducing out-of-pocket costs for families. Use the dropdown to explore how financial aid has changed across different academic years.

Highest Average Aid ~$79K
Lowest Average Aid ~$44K
Aid Difference $35K
04

Admissions vs Enrollments: Understanding Yield Rates

Not all admitted students choose to enroll. This mirrored visualization reveals which schools successfully convert admissions into enrollments.

This chart uses a unique mirrored layout with a center dividing line. Admitted students extend to the left, while enrolled students extend to the right. Each school has its own row, with bars expanding outward from the middle and exact numbers displayed alongside. The visualization uses one color family with lighter and darker shades for visual consistency. As the page loads, bars animate outward, and hovering over any bar reveals exact counts and the calculated yield percentage. This makes it easy to see which schools convert the most admitted students into enrolled students.

Key Finding

Understanding Ivy League affordability requires looking beyond sticker prices to examine financial aid patterns, admission selectivity, and enrollment reality. While published costs appear similar across all eight schools, the actual experience varies dramatically based on how much aid each school offers, how competitive admission has become, and which schools students ultimately choose to attend. Families need this complete, multi-dimensional picture to make truly informed college decisions.

About This Project

This visualization project analyzes Common Data Set information from all eight Ivy League institutions over multiple years. Our goal is to help prospective students and families make more informed college decisions by examining the complete picture: costs, financial aid, admission selectivity, and enrollment patterns.

Data Sources

All data comes from official Common Data Set reports published by each institution.
