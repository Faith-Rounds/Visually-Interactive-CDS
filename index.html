The Complete Picture

Understanding Ivy League affordability requires looking beyond sticker prices to examine financial aid patterns, admission selectivity, and enrollment reality.

While published costs appear remarkably similar, the actual experience varies dramatically based on aid packages, competitive admissions, and where students choose to enroll.

About This Project

This visualization project analyzes Common Data Set information from all eight Ivy League institutions over five years (2020-2025). Our goal is to help prospective students and families make more informed college decisions by examining the complete picture: costs, financial aid, admission selectivity, and enrollment patterns.

Data Sources

All data comes from official Common Data Set reports published by each institution, supplemented by institutional research offices and publicly available admissions data.

Methodology

We collected and standardized CDS data across all schools and years, focusing on cost of attendance, financial aid patterns, admission statistics, and enrollment data. All visualizations are built using D3.js to ensure interactivity and data accuracy.
